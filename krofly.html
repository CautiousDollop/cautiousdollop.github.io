To get started:
-Tap the "Set Destination" button and input your target’s latitude and longitude. You can also give it a custom name for easy reference later.
Examples of Latitude and Longitude Coordinates:

Elora, Ontario → 43.6846, -80.4308 - Paraty, Brazil → -23.2221, -44.7176 - Chefchaouen, Morocco → 35.1688, -5.2636
Dinant, Belgium → 50.2600, 4.9110 - Oia, Greece → 36.4620, 25.3755 - Setenil de las Bodegas, Spain → 36.8629, -5.1813
Portree, Scotland → 57.4125, -6.1936 - Coles Bay, Australia → -42.1205, 148.2896 - Takayama, Japan → 36.1468, 137.2510


-Open the menu (top right corner) to save the current destination either as your default location or add it to your favorites list for future use.
-The center of the screen displays a minimalistic 16-point compass that uses your phone’s GPS to show your destination’s direction relative to your current location. Above the compass, you'll see:
-Your current latitude and longitude
-The distance to your destination
-The top right corner shows your current azimuth — that’s the angle relative to magnetic north — giving you a quick idea of which way you're facing at any time.
